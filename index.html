Getting Started: OpenRCT2 Online Tutorial

New to OpenRCT2? This walkthrough helps get you from an empty park to your first profitable rides. For deeper learning, visit the OpenRCT2 site.

1) Start a Scenario & Pause

  • Pick a beginner-friendly scenario and pause immediately.
  • Scan the map for paths, guest flow, and room to expand.
  • Use the overview map to plan your first “hub” area.

2) First Guests: Paths & Essentials

  • Lay down paths from the park entrance into a central hub.
  • Place basics early: Restrooms, Food/Drink, and Benches/Bins.
  • Hire at least one Handyman to keep the park clean.

3) Your First Ride

  • Start with a gentle ride (e.g., carousel, mini coaster, or transport ride).
  • Make sure the ride has an entrance and exit connected to paths.
  • Open the ride and set a reasonable ticket price.
  • Add a few lights/scenery nearby to boost park rating.

4) Profits & Park Rating

  • Adjust prices based on guest thoughts (“too expensive” / “good value”).
  • Keep paths clean—trash tanks your rating fast.
  • Add more rides with variety: thrill + gentle + transport.
  • Build scenery around key rides for higher excitement and reputation.

5) Saves & Sharing Parks

  • Save progress any time; browser version supports import/export.
  • Keep milestone saves (e.g., “Year 1 stable”, “Coaster expansion”).

Tip: Save before big coaster builds so you can iterate quickly.

6) Learn More

Once you’ve mastered basics, try advanced coaster design, micro-management, and scenario goals.

  • Browse tips and docs on the OpenRCT2 website.
  • Experiment with different ride types and guest path layouts.
